Arteaga

The location of this municipality is in the vicinity of the Sierra Madre Oriental, which allows it to be part of the Cumbres National Park and to be an immediate neighbour of the Nuevo Leon Capital, to the south, at the surroundings of this magical town there are art galleries, decorations, hotels. Restaurants and traditional candy stores, its typical dish consists of roasted ping in red chili sauce with rice and "poisoned" beans.

History

The founding of Arteaga dates from 1580, when the San Isidro de las Palomas ranch was established, which over time became the current municipal seat. In November 2012 Arteaga was named "Pueblo Mágico", thanks to the beauty of its mountains, its history and its traditions. This town received its name as a tribute to Jose María Arteaga, one of the most prominent liberals who participated in the defense of national sovereignty against the American invasion and later the French invasion, which is why Maximiliano had him shot in October 1865.

Places to Visit

In Arteaga you can do activities such as Camping in Monterreal Cabins, visiting the Alameda Venustiano Carranza, Monterreal golf course, San Isidro Canyon, Carbonera, the Lilies, Carranza House, Horsetail Waterfall, El Salto Waterfall, Mountain biking, Sledding, Skiing in Bosques de Monterreal, The Tunal, Sanchez Lagoon, Museum of the Mummies in San Antonio de las Alazanas, within others.

What to Eat

At Arteaga you can try the carnitas, wheat dough empanadas and enchiladas bathed with spicy sauce and slices of onion or lettuce. As typical drinks, Arteaga offers the famous fruit liqueurs, mainly apple and quince.
