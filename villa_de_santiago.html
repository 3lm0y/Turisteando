Villa de Santiago

The location of this municipality is in the Vinicius of the Sierra Madre Oriental, which allows it to be part of the Cumbres National Park and to be an immediate neighbor of the Nuevo Leon capital, to the south, at the surroundings, of this magical town there are art galleries, decoration, hotels, restaurants, and traditional candy stores, its typical dish consists of a roasted pig in red chili sauce with rice and “poisoned” beans.

History

Its current name comes from its old name, founded in 1648 by Diego Rodriguez de Montemayor, "Valle de Santiago de Guajuco", Santiago is located just 33 km away from Monterrey, which makes it ideal for the weekends. The first town registered was in 1745, but in reality, it is said that it was in 1690, but due to a fire caused by theft there are lack of documents, which can't testify to the event.

Places to Visit

In Santiago you can visit the Museo de Historia de Santiago, where you will find out the ideal place to know about the past of this "pueblito magico", and its myths and legends, you can also visit. The "Cascada Cola de Caballo" where you can go and see the beautiful 25 meter waterfall, and also you can visit the "Parroquia de Santiago Apostol" which was founded in 1854, in dedication to the apostol Santiago.

What to Eat

In Santiago you can enjoy delicious plates such as barbeque tacos, "turcos" that are empanadas with pork meat, barbeque ribs with pumpkins and corn, and "caldillo" dried beef broth, making all these dishes perfect to eat and to be satisfied at the end.
