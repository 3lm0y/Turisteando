Linares

Linares is at the Southeast of the Monterrey city, in the plain coast of Mexico, this magical town is a visual attractive with an amazing architecture, temperatures are quite extreme, being the annual average 22°C, 15°C in winter and even getting to 36°C in extreme heat conditions.

History

Its current name comes from its old name of the town of San Felipe de Linares, founded on April 10, 1712. It is named in memory of the thirty-fifth viceroy Fernando de Alencastre, Duke of Linares, establishing itself as a city on May 19, 1777. Linares is located southeast of the city of Monterrey, in the Gulf Coastal Plain region. Its limits are to the north with the municipalities of Hualahuises and Montemorelos; to the south and east with the State of Tamaulipas and to the west with the municipalities of Galeana and Iturbide.

Places to Visit

At Linares you can visit the Ex Hacienda de Guadalupe [Faculty of Earth Sciences, Autonomous University of Nuevo Leon], Botica Morelos, Arteaga Main Square, the Colonial Aqueduct, the Cathedral of San Felipe Apostol and the Linares Casino.

What to Eat

The gastronomy of Linares Nuevo Leon includes a variety of: Dried meat, "machacado", sauced "cabrito al pastor", roasted cuts of meat, tacos "agachados", "tortas compuestas", "glorias", "cajetas", "marquetas", "encanelados", "besos de indios" and "natillas".
